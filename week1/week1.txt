**Scenario:**
A logistics company needs to deliver three high-priority packages: 
a fragile glass item, a temperature-sensitive food item, 
and a flammable chemical container. 
Only one item can be transported at a time in a drone, 
and certain items cannot be left together:

The chemical container and food must not be left together unsupervised.

The glass item and chemical container are safe together.

The food and glass are safe together.

Task:
Design an algorithm that ensures all three items are delivered safely across the city from the warehouse to a storage facility.

**Activity 1: Business Logistics Problem (Solution)**


All items at warehouse

Carry Food to Storage

Return Empty

Carry Chemical to Storage

Bring Food back to Warehouse

Carry Glass to Storage

Return Empty

Carry Food to Storage

All items safely at Storage Facility

**Activity 2 - Chocolate Sorting → Customer Segmentation Scenario:**
You have a bag of customer survey results containing responses from two types of customers:
 B2B and B2C. Each result is labeled only after opening it.

Task:
Design an algorithm to sort customer responses into two different piles: B2B and B2C.



**Activity 2 - Chocolate Sorting → Customer Segmentation (Solution)**

WHILE survey_responses IS NOT empty:

    REMOVE response FROM survey_responses

    IF response.customer_type == 'B2B':

        ADD response TO b2b_list

    ELSE:

        ADD response TO b2c_list
